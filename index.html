Unit 1: Data

In computer science, data is represented in bits and bytes. A bit is a 0 or 1, and a byte is a sequence of 8 bits. Data can be analog (continuous) or digital (discrete). Compression can be lossless (no data lost) or lossy (some data is lost). Metadata is data about data, like file information.

Unit 2: Computer Systems and Networks

A computer network is a group of devices that send and receive data. They use protocols (rules for communication). Data is sent as packets, guided by routers. The internet works because of IP addresses, TCP, and HTTP. The system is fault tolerant and scalable, meaning it keeps working even if parts fail and can grow to support billions of devices.

Censorship & Free Speech Online

The internet gives people a place to share ideas, but sometimes governments or companies limit what can be posted or seen. This is called censorship. Free speech online is about the balance between letting people say what they want and protecting others from harmful content.
